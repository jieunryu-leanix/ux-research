Key Insight 1
ADR-as-dossier, not ADR-as-record

All four customers expect a connected hub: diagrams, principles, fact sheets, and approval evidence in one document. LeanIX models a single-decision record. The gap is structural.

Key Insight 2
The approval model is broken for governed organizations

Multi-stakeholder routing, custom outcome statuses, and immutable audit trails after approval are compliance requirements — not preferences. One approval in production has already failed.

Key Insight 3
Architecture principles have no home in LeanIX

Three of four customers named principles independently as the governance layer they can't manage. Customer to LeanIX: "We are waiting for you."

Hypotheses Validation

H1
● Partial
The primary bottleneck to ADR adoption is discoverability
Not confirmed as the main bottleneck. All four customers navigate by direct link or portal. The real blockers are upstream (no pre-draft workflow) and downstream (no approval mechanism, no audit trail). H1 is not applicable to pre-adoption customers like Safran.
H2
⊘ Retired
"Create ADR → Update Diagram" is the natural sequence
Contradicted in all four sessions. DHL: Diagram→Council Review→ADR. Pernod Ricard: Diagram and ADR co-authored in parallel. Toyota Kreditbank: Parallel. Safran: ArchiMate→PPT→review→ADR. The ADR is a governance output, not an input. H2 should not appear in future research instruments.
H3
● Partial
Low diagram embedding is due to non-obviousness
Confirmed but more complex. Pernod Ricard: team was shown the feature, still uses links — habit/UX friction, not awareness. Toyota Kreditbank: actively embeds, no discoverability barrier. Safran: pre-adoption. DHL: conceptual barrier ("not every diagram is a formal decision").
H4
✓ Confirmed
ADR creation is a multi-session, cross-team workflow
Strongly confirmed across all sessions with escalating evidence. Toyota Kreditbank: ARB + IT security + InfoSec + architecture leads, parallel domain sign-offs. Most complex multi-stakeholder process seen. Re-engagement nudges and collaboration features are structural requirements.
H5
✓ Confirmed
Status updates and content edits are cognitively separate tasks
Strongly confirmed. Every customer treats status change as a distinct governance act. Toyota Kreditbank: "approval state and content editing are not separated" is the stated core pain. DHL: approval is tracked entirely outside LeanIX. Pernod Ricard: "Proposed" change is a deliberate gate managed by a different person.
H6
✓ Confirmed
Fact Sheets are a natural entry point for discovering related ADRs
Confirmed for advanced users. Pernod Ricard and Toyota Kreditbank actively use the Resources section to navigate to linked decisions. List length is becoming a usability issue for high-traffic applications at Pernod Ricard.
H7
● Partial
Users expect ADR impact to propagate to linked Fact Sheets
Partially confirmed. Pernod Ricard's Deborah manually propagates impact — she wants automation with a review gate. Toyota Kreditbank wants principle compliance visible on fact sheets. Expectation exists; the question is automated vs. mediated propagation.

New Insights

Insight 1

The ADR-as-dossier mental model is universal and conflicts with current feature design

Every customer — regardless of maturity — described the ADR as a navigation hub, not a single decision record. The mental model: one document connecting diagrams (current and target), linked fact sheets, principles followed or excepted, initiative context, and approval evidence.

Safran calls it an "ASR" and describes it as a dossier. Toyota Kreditbank's Sabrina proposed the "Word to PDF" model unprompted: a collaborative working document that generates a locked artifact on approval. Pernod Ricard manually builds this hub across diagrams, fact sheets, and to-dos. DHL wants LeanIX as the repository for every architecture deliverable.

The current ADR feature is a text document with a status. The gap is structural.

"Going into an ASR, you can go to application fact sheet, initiative fact sheet, and then going deeper into the GANT of the project." — JP Matton, Safran
"You have the Word document, people are working on it... and once you say yes, it's fine — generate the PDF and nobody can touch it." — Sabrina Oberdörfer, Toyota Kreditbank
Insight 2

Approval is a multi-stakeholder, domain-routed governance process — not a status field

LeanIX's single "Accepted" status does not map to any customer's approval process. DHL: council reviews integration, BI, data protection, security separately — approval is outside LeanIX entirely. Pernod Ricard: two reviewers, no notification when "Proposed." Toyota Kreditbank: IT security + InfoSec + architecture each approve their domain — a wrong-role approval has already caused a compliance incident in production. Safran: ARB must formally accept exceptions to principles.

Three specific requirements emerge: (1) domain-routed approval; (2) custom outcome statuses ("accepted with exception," "accepted with risk," "accepted with follow-up"); (3) immutable audit trail with change log and role attribution — a compliance necessity for regulated industries.

"I see a last updated 11 days ago, no idea who it was." — Sabrina Oberdörfer, Toyota Kreditbank (on missing audit trail)
"It's not prevented by the system. That's the problem." — Sabrina Oberdörfer, Toyota Kreditbank (on wrong-role approval)
"We need more process statuses before the draft stage." — DHL participant (on pre-draft governance phases invisible in LeanIX)
Insight 3

Architecture principles are the missing governance layer — named by 3 of 4 customers independently

DHL: principles are group-wide standards every project must follow, but cannot be linked to ADRs or analyzed. Safran: principles live as short labels in ArchiMate. When asked where they're stored in LeanIX: "We are waiting for you." JP wants portfolio queries: "Show me all the applications not in accordance with Cloud First." Toyota Kreditbank: Sabrina has the most complete model — 15 principles, each ADR must state which are followed or excepted, ARB must approve exceptions, governance report by compliance rate per principle.

"We are waiting for you." — JP Matton, Safran
"Show me all the applications that are not in accordance with Cloud First." — JP Matton, Safran
"Cloud first is our principle, but Kazakhstan has a government law — you can't do anything about it. You just document the exception and the Architecture Review Board approves it." — Sabrina Oberdörfer, Toyota Kreditbank
Insight 4

The pre-ADR gap is real — currently filled by Jira, ArchiMate, or nothing

Every customer has a phase between "someone has an idea" and "ADR is in Draft" that LeanIX does not support. Pernod Ricard uses ServiceNow→Jira→LeanIX. Jira exists solely because LeanIX has no pre-Draft triage step. DHL uses Excel for project requests. The implication: not that LeanIX should become a task manager, but that the ADR status model needs earlier stages ("Intake," "Triage," "In Review") that map to pre-Draft governance phases already happening in customers' organizations.

"We have a kind of overlap [between Jira and LeanIX]. Then the question is, do we still need the GER?" — Deborah Aicardi Douchin, Pernod Ricard
Insight 5

AI document generation from LeanIX data is the clearest AI use case — not querying

Safran: "Please create me my PowerPoint based on this template for the application MS Dynamics" — template-driven generation from fact sheet data. Toyota Kreditbank's Sabrina already does this manually: Copilot records the ARB meeting, she asks it to extract ADR content, hands the draft to the solution architect. This is live today, outside LeanIX. Pernod Ricard: immediate positive reaction to the diagram-to-ADR AI generation demo. "If we use the functionality you just showed me in the diagram, that could solve it." The highest-demand AI capability: given existing LeanIX data, generate a structured document draft. The MCP integration opportunity is already real for Toyota Kreditbank.

"We are spending more time on documenting what we decided than on deciding what to do." — JP Matton, Safran
"Please create me my PowerPoint based on this template for the application MS Dynamics." — Fong Visdeloup, Safran
"I ask Copilot to generate the relevant information for the Architecture Decision record and provide it to the solution architect." — Sabrina Oberdörfer, Toyota Kreditbank (live AI workflow today)
"If we use the functionality you just showed me in the diagram, that could solve it." — Deborah Aicardi Douchin, Pernod Ricard
Insight 6

Export for non-LeanIX stakeholders is a persistent adoption blocker

C-level stakeholders, external reviewers, and compliance officers are not LeanIX users. Safran: needs PowerPoint-compatible output — PDF and Markdown are insufficient. Pernod Ricard: the "Decision Paper" (PPT deck) remains the authoritative presentation artifact alongside the LeanIX ADR — both must exist. DHL: architecture deliverables are currently PowerPoints; goal is to produce the same document from LeanIX. Current Markdown export does not address the executive communication need.

"We cannot export Architecture Decision." — Fong Visdeloup, Safran
"Always, always, always need the diagram. We are quite strict on this one." — Deborah Aicardi Douchin, Pernod Ricard (on mandatory diagram + Decision Paper)

Open Questions for Next Research Round

  1. What does a legally defensible audit trail require for regulated industries — field-level diff log, or is role + timestamp sufficient?
  2. Should the pre-ADR workspace be a separate fact sheet type, a draft-phase state, or a template that generates a locked ADR on promotion?
  3. How many LeanIX ADR customers are in regulated industries — is the audit trail gap a segment-level retention risk?
  4. What specific statuses do customers need in the pre-Draft phase? Can a standard set (Intake, In Triage, Assigned, Kicked Off) satisfy most cases?
  5. Where should architecture principles live: custom fact sheet, new native fact sheet type, or ADR template field with principle-as-object linking?
  6. If LeanIX exposed an MCP endpoint for ADR creation, would Toyota Kreditbank route Copilot output directly into LeanIX instead of copy-pasting?
  7. How should group-level ADRs (Safran central DSI) relate to company-level ADRs across 11 entities — hierarchy, inheritance, or flat linking?
