1. How Do Users Navigate to Architecture Decisions?

Based on Amplitude data from the first week of May and ongoing dashboard tracking.

Key Findings

  • Direct access dominates (~94%). Most users navigate to the Decision list directly via session start — bookmarks or direct URL. No organic discovery funnel drives the majority of traffic.
  • Factsheet resource tab is the #1 organic entry point (~1.2%). Of the remaining ~5.8% organic traffic, Factsheet and Diagram-based navigation are nearly equal, making Diagrams a co-equal organic channel.
  • Diagram-based entry is growing rapidly. Over 12 weeks: Diagram 819 events vs. Factsheet 869. In last 4 weeks: Diagrams (321) nearly match total FS ingress (350) — a fast-emerging primary organic channel.
  • Status updates represent a distinct editing pattern. Of 279 users who clicked the edit button, ~32% (~88 users) performed a status update as their first action. These should be tracked separately from "authoring" activity.
  • ~94% of saves happen within the first hour of opening the edit flow — editing is near-synchronous with opening.

2.1 Diagram & ADR

Key takeaway: Weak bridge between diagram and decision. Only 10% of ADR users embedded a diagram; but 43% of ADR creators later saved a diagram — "Create ADR → Update Diagram" appears to be the dominant pattern.

  • ADR users are power users who actively work across both features. Median time between ADR interaction and diagram save: ~2 days.
  • The relationship is asymmetric: Diagrams are a broad entry point (13K users), ADRs are high-intent and niche (666 users) — but ADR users are far more likely to loop back into diagrams.
  • Discoverability is the primary bottleneck, not UX friction. Only 2% of diagram-savers subsequently create an ADR. ADR completion once started is healthy (~53%), so the problem is awareness, not workflow difficulty.
  • Only 10% of ADR users embedded a diagram in their decision — the two features are used in parallel rather than integrated.
  • Multi-session workflow: Median time from saving a diagram to creating an ADR is ~6 days — requires in-product re-engagement nudges.

2.2 Transformation & ADR

Key takeaway: Diagrams and ADR are tightly coupled (43% crossover), but Transformations is largely a separate workflow with little overlap with either.

  • After saving a Transformation, users go to Diagrams (~15%), not ADRs (~1%). Practical workflow: Transformation → Diagram (visual iteration), not Transformation → ADR (decision formalization).
  • Users who commit transformation changes are more likely to create an ADR (8%), double the rate of casual visitors (4.1%).
  • The full Diagram → ADR → Transformation journey is used by only ~21 users (0.16%) — a tiny but highly engaged power-user segment.
  • ~5–7 day gap between steps confirms this is a planned, multi-session workflow.

  • 28% arrive via documents-decision-open — returning to a previously opened decision.

3. Retention & Adoption

Key takeaway: Decision completion is stable (~46%); template completion has significantly improved (~53%); template WAU is down sharply — monitor.

  • Decision List has a small but loyal recurring user base. Steep initial drop (100% → ~15% at W1), but flat retention W1–W12 signals stickiness for engaged users.
  • Decision create-to-save: 46.1% — stable vs. May's ~45–50%.
  • Decision edit-to-save: 83.1% — once users start editing, they almost always finish.
  • Template create-to-save: 53.3% — improved from ~47%.
  • Template open-to-save: 53.3% — major improvement from 33.6%.
  • FS-origin save rate: 19.7% — improved from 10.6%.
  • Template WAU: 85 users, −24.8% WoW — needs monitoring.

5. Next Steps

Improve discoverability

  • Enhance linking decision widget to support MCP-gen ADR as well as manual creation & linking
  • Modularize the ADR linking/generation pattern from Diagram (TAP Sapphire) and expand to FS, AI chat, and dashboard

Make it more enterprise-ready

Strategize template layout: Confluence-like text field for Narrative-driven EAs & Jira-like metadata fields for Process-driven EAs — one led by AI agent, the other by Automation.

Connect persona across journey

Align roles and permission controls across services (FS, Diagram, Transform, AD).
